The Caesar Cipher

"Technique"

The caesar cipher is a very old form of cryptograhy. To encrypt something using this method, one must match each letter of their alphabet to a number.

Example:

  1. A = 1
  2. B = 2
  3. C = 3
  4. D = 4
  5. E = 5
  6. D = 6
  7. ...
  8. X = 25
  9. Y = 27
  10. Z = 26


After this, one can apply a shift to the alphabet.


Example, where shift is 3
newLetterIndex = (i + 3) modulas 25; and i is the index of the letter of the English alphabet. (starting from 1)

  1. A = 4
  2. B = 5
  3. C = 6
  4. D = 7
  5. E = 8
  6. D = 9
  7. ...

  10. Z = 3
